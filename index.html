MyDocNotes

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 May 2026

MyDocNotes stores everything on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere, and no one else can see your information.

1. What information does MyDocNotes store?

MyDocNotes only stores information that you enter directly:

  • Your first name (used for the greeting on the home screen)
  • Appointment details: doctor name, type, date, time and location
  • Audio recordings of your appointments
  • Notes you type or speak during a visit
  • Questions you add before a visit

All of this is stored on your phone only. It is never uploaded to any server or shared with anyone.

2. Where is my information stored?

Everything is stored directly on your device:

  • Appointments, notes and questions are saved in a private database on your phone.
  • Audio recordings are saved in a dedicated folder on your device. This folder is private to MyDocNotes and is not accessible to other apps.
  • Files you choose to export (PDF summaries or MP3 recordings) are saved to your Downloads folder.

You can delete any appointment, recording or note at any time from within the app. Deletion is permanent and immediate.

If you uninstall MyDocNotes, your appointments, notes and questions will be permanently deleted. Audio recordings saved to your private folder may also be deleted depending on your device. Files already saved to your Downloads folder will remain.

3. Does MyDocNotes share my information?

No. MyDocNotes does not share your information with anyone. The app has no servers and no internet connection by default.

If you choose to export a PDF summary or MP3 recording to your Downloads folder, you control who sees those files. MyDocNotes plays no role in sending them — you choose when and how to share using your phone's own tools, such as WhatsApp or email.

4. No analytics or tracking

MyDocNotes contains no analytics, no crash reporting, and no tracking of any kind. We do not collect any data about how you use the app. No third-party SDKs or advertising libraries are included.

5. Google Calendar (optional)

If you choose to enable Google Calendar sync, your appointment dates, times, doctor names and locations are shared with Google to create calendar events. Your audio recordings, notes and questions are never shared with Google.

You can turn Google Calendar sync on or off at any time in Settings. Turning it off will stop future appointments from being added to your calendar, but will not remove appointments already created there.

6. Recording consent

Before every recording, MyDocNotes shows a consent message for you to show your doctor. Recording a conversation without the other person's consent may be against the law in your country. Always get your doctor's agreement before recording. MyDocNotes is not responsible for how the recording feature is used.

7. Microphone

MyDocNotes only uses your microphone when you start a recording or use voice input for notes. It does not listen in the background at any other time.

8. Reminders and notifications

If you enable appointment reminders, MyDocNotes schedules notifications on your device. No information leaves your device to do this — it is handled entirely by your phone's built-in notification system.

9. Children

MyDocNotes is designed for adults managing their medical appointments. It is not intended for use by children under 16.

10. GDPR and your rights (European users)

MyDocNotes is designed to process as little personal data as possible. All data is stored locally on your device and is under your full control at all times.

Since MyDocNotes has no servers and collects no data centrally, most GDPR obligations do not apply. However, if you have any privacy concerns you can contact us at the address below and we will respond within 30 days.

11. Changes to this policy

If we update this privacy policy, the new version will be included in the next app update. We will not make changes that reduce your privacy protections without clear notice.
